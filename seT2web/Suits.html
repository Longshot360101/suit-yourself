Suits

There are many different types of suits and styling, they all serve a different purpose and occasion. However this will only cover suits that are for the modern age.

Suit Styles

The most well known styling are British, American and Italian.

The British Suit

The characteristics of a traditional British styled suit are that they are single breasted with two buttons, have two vents at the side of the jacket, a tapered waist and higher armholes.

The American Suit

The characteristics of a traditional American styled suit are that they are single breasted with three buttons, have a single vent down the middle of the jacket, no shoulder padding and have a loose fit.

The Italian Suit

The characteristics of a traditional Italian styled suit are that they have no flaps on the pockets, no vents, padded shoulders and that they have a very slim and tailored waist.

Suit Components

The main components of a suit are buttons, lapels, vents and pleats.

Buttons

Buttons are located on two parts of the suit, the main front area of the suit and on the sleeves. The buttons on the main area of the suit can either be configured to a single or double breasted suit. A double breasted suit is usually seen as more formal than a single breasted suit however a double breasted suit can still be worn in a less formal setting. The buttons on the sleeves of the jacket serve a decorative purpose more than a functional purpose. The buttons on the sleeve can be used to roll up the sleeves however this is rarely done. To add, the buttons on suits that are bought off the rack usually don't have any functionality and are purely cosmetic.

Single breasted on left and double breasted on right.

Lapels

On the jacket of the suit there are three types of lapels that will be seen, notched, peak and shawl. The notched lapel is the most common type, and it can be found on many off the rack suits. It is also versatile for any occasion. The peak lapel is reserved for slightly more formal settings however it can be used less formally as well. The shawl lapel is the least common type of lapel as it is seen as very formal and it is almost exclusive on tuxedos.

Vents

A suit vent is a vertical slit that extends upwards from the bottom hem on the backside of a suit jacket or blazer. There are three option to choose from,single or double vent or ventless.

Pleats

Pleats are folds in the fabric of clothing and on pleated dress pants they are found at the front immediately under the waistband, on either side of the fly. Pleats either come in a single fold or a double fold and you can choose between forward or reverse folded.
