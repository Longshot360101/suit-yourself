Shirts

The shirt is the second most important aspect of a formal attire as it is the main complement of a Suit, Blazer or Sport Coat. Just like a suit the dress shirt has many aspects of it that can be customised and tuned to your liking.

Shirt Components

The main components of a shirt are the collar and cuffs.

Collar

There are two types of collars, the wing and the turned down collar. The wing collar is seen on very formal dress shirts. This type of collar stands stiffly with small points pressed to stick out. The turned down collar are more common on shirts generally, there are many variations of this collar but the main versions are the point or spread/cutaway.

Cuffs

When it comes down the cuffs of a shirt there are two types that each have variation on them, the single or double cuff. The single cuff is the most common type that is seen on shirts. The double cuff or 'french cuffs' are meant for formal occasions denoted when the fabric folds back on itself to create a thicker cuff. This version requires cufflinks to be worn in order to keep the cuff in shape.
