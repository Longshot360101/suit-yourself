Blazers and Sport Coats

Alternatives to suits if less attention is what is desired or if the occasion is more casual.

Blazers

Blazers are defined by both their colour and the buttons that are present on it.

Colour

The colour of a blazer is often a solid colour, the most common being blue or black which adds to its formality.

Buttons

A blazer is usually made with buttons that are either silver, gold or brass.

Sport Coats

A sport coat is distinguished from a blazer through its patterned cloth, fabric and weight. A sport coat is the most casual of the three jacket types.

Patterns

Since a sport coat is a more casual option it can be customised more in terms of patterns to show off one's individual style. The most common patterns to see on a sport coat are: glen check, windowpane, herringbone and houndstooth.

Fabric

The most common fabrics that can be found on a sport coat are: tweed, houndstooth, mohair and hopsack. Tweed is one of the most recognisable materials for jackets that are worn during sporting events. Its main features are that it has a rough feel and is best worn in cool weather. It is associated with traditional British style and comes in different styles such as Donegal or herringbone. Houndstooth is a busy print that makes an impressionable statement, and it has a low flexibility in terms of styling, thus it better suited for those who already possess multiple sport coats. Mohair is made from lightweight and breathable fabric used for summer sport jackets, it is distinguished by a beautiful sheen and smooth texture, this makes it more formal, thus is less casual than other fabrics. Hopsack is a loosely woven fabric with thicker yarns that makes it a more casual and breathable option for sport coats. It also has a textured appearance and hangs loosely which makes it popular in hot weather.

Weight

The weight of the jacket determines how much the fabric is used. Weight is most often measured in ounces/grams, there are multiple weights that are available and they depend on what type of weather it currently is. A lighter weight means that it is suited for warmer weather while a heavier suit is suited for colder weather.
